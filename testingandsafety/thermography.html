Thermography

Thermography is used as a proactive diagnostic tool, which allows the detection of developing faults, before they cause system failure or worse problems such as an electrical fire. Thermal imaging measures the radiant heat pattern which is emitted from electrical components in systems such as building power distribution or plant equipment. All work is carried out to AS/NZ Standards.

Benefits of Thermography

  • Reduce risk of blackouts and system failures
  • Reduce risk of productivity downtime
  • Increase safety of staff and customers
  • Reduce risk of electrical fire

Potential Cost Savings

  • Replace only faulty components, rather than whole sections of a system
  • Reduce unexpected maintenance costs
  • Schedule proactive maintenance, rather than pay additional costs for emergency or unscheduled repairs
